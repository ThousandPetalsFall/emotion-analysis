# 01. System Roles

## Core Positioning

The system should not treat `mood-diary` and `emotion-analysis` as two unrelated apps.

They form one path:

1. leave daily traces
2. discover repeating patterns
3. enter a deeper conversation at the right moment

## `mood-diary` Role

`mood-diary` is the daily entry layer.

It should be:

- lightweight
- emotionally safe
- usable even when the user has very little energy
- suitable for high-frequency use

Its main job is not to solve the whole problem. Its main job is to capture signals:

- today's mood
- short reflections
- recurring words such as "empty", "numb", "tired", "no meaning"
- whether the user still feels alive in daily life

## `emotion-analysis` Role

`emotion-analysis` is the deep-guidance layer.

It should be:

- lower frequency
- more intentional
- more structured
- more reflective

Its job is to help the user move from surface discomfort to underlying mechanisms, for example:

- "I got what I wanted, so why do I still feel empty?"
- "My life became more convenient, but why not more alive?"
- "Am I resting, or am I hiding?"
- "Am I unhappy, or have I simply gone numb?"

## Division of Labor

`mood-diary` should answer:

- What am I feeling today?
- What words keep showing up in my daily life?
- Is there a repeated pattern worth noticing?

`emotion-analysis` should answer:

- What may be behind this repeated pattern?
- Which psychological or existential mechanism may be operating?
- What small shift can help me re-enter a more felt life?

## Success Standard

The system succeeds when a user can move from:

- "I only know that something feels off"

to:

- "I can name what is happening to me"

and then to:

- "I know one small next step that may help me feel life again"
